It had been months since I'd last seen work and weeks since I'd last seen money. Every favor called in, every dribble of luck squeezed out, and tomorrow: the landlord. I slept poorly.

Half-asleep, I heard a voice.

At once a stillness came upon me: not contentment, certainly not happiness, but stillness. The world was an unholy gale against which I could stand for the first time: I was braced against the wind. Solid.

Before I could whisper my thanks, I woke.

I woke transformed; I no longer knew myself. I could not find any meaning in what I saw and felt. I could not even move - I did not know the rules governing my own body. So I set myself to experiment.
